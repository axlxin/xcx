Twitter

  • Register a new app at dev.twitter.com/apps/
  • Fill in the fields for your site accordingly, just be sure to select Browser in Application Type, and set the Callback URL to something likehttp://localhost.com/twitter_login.php (http://localhost/ won't be accepted because it doesn't have a domain name).
  • The callback URL will be: www.yourdomain.com/index.php?route=account/socialconnect/twitter.
  • Finally, select Read & Write. Fill in the captcha, click "Register Application," and accept the Terms of Service.
  • Now fill the form to request a user email address

    Requesting a user’s email address requires your application to be whitelisted by Twitter. To request access, please use this form.

    Once whitelisted, the “Request email addresses from users” checkbox will be available under your app permissions on apps.twitter.com. Privacy Policy URL and Terms of Service URL fields will also be available under settings which are required for email access. If enabled, users will be informed via the oauth/authorize dialog that your app can access their email address.

    Note

    Your app will need to regenerate the user access tokens for previously authenticated users to access their email address.

    Don't define the callback URL, it will be handled by the extension.
    More details: https://dev.twitter.com/rest/reference/get/account/verify_credentials

LinkedIn

  • If you haven't created the applicaiton yet then Create a new app at www.linkedin.com/developer/apps
  • Fill the field properly and you are done, don't mentioned the redirect URL it will be done by the extension it self.
  • In your app detail page select r_emailaddress under "Default Application Permissions" and click update button
  • Enter client ID & Client secret to opencart extension setting area.
  • Once done, activate your application from setting tab by selecting "Live" from Application Status select box
